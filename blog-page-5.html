Picking the Right Keyboard

Jamodin Maliwanag - October 26, 2021

Keyboards have many different customizable options. They all function the same but vary in sizes, prices, “feel”, and look.

Types of Keyboards

Keyboards come in many different types but we will only focus on the main 2 types:

  • Membrane Keyboards
  • Mechanical Keyboards

Membrane Keyboards

This type of keyboard is the average keyboard you can find in an office or school. What makes this keyboard different from a mechanical keyboard is that the keys are not separated. The keys are connected by a conductive layer with circuits and detect when a key is pressed when the conductive layer gets pressed down and completes the circuit. The downside to a membrane keyboard is that it usually can only register 6 key inputs at a time. This can be annoying for fast typers or anyone who has to input multiple keys at once or quickly.

The conductive layer is pressed down and connects the circuit

Mechanical Keyboards

This type of keyboard has grown in popularity, especially for gamers. It works by having separate keys known as switches. These switches are spring-loaded, meaning they spring back up quickly, ready to be pressed down again. This is useful for fast typers and gamers that input multiple keys at a time.

Each key is separate for precise and fast inputs

Keyboard Switches

Switches also come in multiple varieties but we will talk about the main 3 types:

  • Blue Switches
  • Red Switches
  • Brown Switches

Blue Switches

Blue switches are the most well known because of it's audible clicky sounds when pressing keys. They can be quite loud, however they have a nice “click” feel when pressing down a key that lets you know that you have pressed down the key.

Red Switches

Red switches act as an opposite to blue switches. They are usually quieter when pressing keys and they still have a noticeable “click” feel when pressing down a key, but it's less obvious.

Brown Switches

Brown switches are a balance of both blue and red switch properties. It has a less audible “click” sound like the red switches but has a noticeable “click” feel like the blue switches.

6 different types of switches

How Price Varies

With all of the options and customization for keyboards, the price for one can vary. Membrane keyboards are the cheapest while mechanical keyboards are more expensive. However, some mechanical keyboards can be cheap because of:

  • Branding (Ducky one, Corsair, Redragon)
  • Keyboard material (plastic or metal with the latter being more expensive)
  • Custom Keycaps (different colors or design)
  • Wired or wireless (wireless being more expensive)

Summary

If you have a decent budget for a new keyboard, a mechanical keyboard is the best option. The feel of it is better than a membrane keyboard. You do not need your keyboard to have custom keycaps and be wireless, which saves quite a bit of money.
